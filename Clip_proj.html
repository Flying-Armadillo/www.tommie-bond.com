PROJECT NAME

The muntin window is a classic colonial style window, unfortunately the clips that my client was using are 50 years old, brittle, and no longer able to serve their purpose and exact replacements were hard to find. The solution I proposed was to 3D print replacements, as it was cheap and easy to produce. In addition, because of the small size of the clips, the time between prototypes was only seven minutes.
An intact original was measured, and a copy was modelled in 360 Fusion.
The model was exported from 360 Fusion and converted using Flash Print 5.
The initial 3D prototype was printed and test fitted to see if there were any flaws in the design.
The prototype worked well, so about 100 clips were printed in batches of 8.
